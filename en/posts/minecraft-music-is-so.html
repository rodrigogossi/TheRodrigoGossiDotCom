Minecraft's music is so...

Published on: June 7, 2022 Calculating...

These days, while exploring Spotify, I stumbled almost by accident upon a playlist of Minecraft soundtracks. I don't really know why, but I found it so unexpected that I decided to press play. Initially, I thought the songs would be simple, maybe even strange—after all, they were just music from a game I hadn't played in years. I was moved purely by curiosity. To my surprise, that's not what happened. As soon as I pressed play, I felt as if I had been transported to another dimension...

Why is Minecraft's music so... nostalgic?

Minecraft, released in 2011, is a lonely, mysterious adventure game. In it, the player is dropped into an empty world, free to build whatever they want, however they prefer, with no rules or patterns to follow. Just explore, gather materials, and let your imagination soar. That is the incredible essence of the game. Escaping the real world and appearing in a new universe where you can be whoever you want. Want to be a king? Build a castle. Or maybe a farmer? Place some blocks and voilà, a farm appears. Simple as that. If you, like me, discovered Minecraft in your childhood, you realize how the game, despite being simple, is amazing. After all, simplicity also has its complexity.

And that's where Minecraft's music comes in. Have you ever stopped to think that creating music for a game is almost an impossible mission? The soundtrack needs to express the feeling of the game, the atmosphere in which the player is immersed. The piano and other simple instruments manage to perfectly capture the feeling that Minecraft carries: an infinite world in your hands, where you can do whatever you want, but you are alone.

I fondly remember afternoons spent building cities, kingdoms, and houses (watching tutorials on YouTube, haha). Everything was simpler, with no worries. Life was just about playing, taking a warm bath, and sleeping, with the day's problems disappearing at dawn. Every morning was a fresh start. Minecraft manages to encapsulate this melancholic and nostalgic feeling of the time I lived—the only time I really lived intensely. Seeing life with affection, love, creativity, and without fear of reinventing myself, learning and, perhaps, having to learn all over again. Every little thing was new.

Sometimes, when listening to these songs again, I can remember myself as a child, my unique way of looking at the world, that bittersweet simplicity. The dreams, who I wanted to be when I grew up, the happiness in knowing that one day I would make those dreams come true. And then, I grew up. I didn't see the child leaving, just as I couldn't see the waves separating us. I hope one day to reunite with the child I was, to feel that sensitivity again, those living dreams.

Dear child I was: listening to these songs, I still remember you, and how I owe everything I am to you. To you, who were braver, dreaming in such a cruel world, unlike the game, without the power to control. I hope that, on some late afternoon, I will find you, that you will keep writing your stories, painting your drawings, believing in a magical world. That you teach me again what I forgot, tell me bedtime stories, make me forget that the real world doesn't have a creative mode. Dedicated to the Rodrigo I was,

With love, Rodrigo.
